Sector — Warehouse & Distribution

Industrial scale. Commercial discipline.

Distribution centers, fulfillment hubs, and logistics facilities demand construction speed and precision. We bring both — with the safety culture to match.

Who We Serve

The supply chain doesn't wait — neither do we.

Warehouse and distribution construction is driven by throughput. When a facility is offline or under construction, inventory flow stops, costs mount, and supply chain commitments get missed. The contractor you choose determines how much of that risk you absorb.

RJB brings the same accountability we've built in retail construction into industrial environments. Our crews are trained to work safely inside active facilities — around forklifts, racking systems, and ongoing shipping and receiving operations — without slowing down the facility's core function.

We work with retailers expanding their distribution footprint, third-party logistics operators building out new facilities, and e-commerce companies converting existing industrial space into fulfillment operations. The scope changes. The standard doesn't.

What We Build

From tenant improvement to full facility build-out.

01
Tenant Improvements
Turnkey TI scopes that convert shell industrial space into operational distribution and fulfillment facilities. We coordinate with landlords, engineers, and local authorities from permit to punch list.
02
Dock & Door Work
Loading dock construction, dock leveler installation, overhead door replacement, and dock seal systems for high-volume receiving and shipping operations.
03
Office & Mezzanine Build-Outs
Interior office construction, mezzanine additions, breakrooms, and support space built inside active warehouse facilities without disrupting operations below.

Floor Coatings & Marking
Epoxy flooring, safety striping, equipment zones, and pedestrian path marking systems handled by our coatings crews. High-traffic industrial surfaces done right the first time.

Expansions & Additions

06
Ongoing Facility Maintenance
Scheduled maintenance and capital improvement programs for distribution facilities that need a single reliable partner for recurring construction and repair needs.
What Makes Warehouse Hard

Challenges we've already solved.

Active Facility Operations

Construction inside a functioning distribution center requires coordination at every level — from scheduling around receiving windows to maintaining clear forklift lanes while building in adjacent zones.

Safety in Industrial Environments

Working around forklifts, racking systems, and heavy equipment means our safety protocols have to be built for an industrial context, not adapted from a retail playbook. Our crews are trained and certified accordingly.

Timeline Pressure

Supply chain commitments don't move for construction. We pre-plan every project to ensure our schedule matches the facility's operational needs — not the other way around.

Coordination Complexity

Industrial projects involve equipment vendors, racking contractors, fire protection engineers, and local code authorities. We coordinate all of it so the facility operator doesn't have to manage a dozen separate conversations.
