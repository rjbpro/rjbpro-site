Building for Curbside: What the Pickup Era Demands

April 15, 2026 · RJB Contracting

Curbside pickup moved from a pandemic-era convenience to a baseline customer expectation. For retailers, meeting that expectation across an existing store fleet is as much a construction challenge as an operations one.

Designing for the handoff

A smooth curbside experience depends on physical changes to the store: dedicated parking and clear wayfinding signage, a staging area near the front-of-house, and sometimes new exterior power and data for lockers, kiosks, or order screens. Each of these touches the building, and each has to work the same way at every location to be worth the investment.

Doing it without losing selling days

The hard part isn't the scope. It's executing it in a store that never closes. Open-store sequencing, after-hours work, and tight phasing let crews complete the conversion while customers keep shopping. That coordination is where an experienced retail contractor earns its keep.

Build once, scale everywhere

Omnichannel only pays off at portfolio scale. The retailers getting it right treat curbside as a repeatable construction program (one standard, rolled out location by location) rather than a series of one-off projects.
