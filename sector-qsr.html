Sector: QSR

Speed to open is the only metric that matters

QSR construction lives and dies on schedule. We've built the systems, the crews, and the subcontractor relationships to deliver on time, every time, at scale.

Who We Serve

QSR construction demands a different kind of contractor

In the quick-service restaurant business, every day a location isn't open is revenue that doesn't come back. The pressure is real, the timelines are tight, and the brand standards are non-negotiable. Most contractors treat QSR projects like any other commercial job. We don't.

RJB has remodeled high-volume drive-through operations without missing a day of service and managed national rollout programs across dozens of locations simultaneously. We know what a QSR brand expects, and we know how to meet those expectations consistently, not just once.

Whether you're a franchise operator building your first standalone unit or a brand-side construction director managing a 50-unit remodel program, RJB has the depth to execute at your scale.

What Makes QSR Hard

Challenges we've already solved

Schedule Compression

QSR construction timelines are often set by real estate deals, not construction reality. We pre-plan every phase so the crew hits the ground running and doesn't stop until the ribbon is cut.

Brand Compliance

QSR brands have exacting prototype standards. Our project managers are fluent in reading and executing brand packages, and our teams don't improvise on details that matter to the franchisor.

Operating Through Remodels

Many QSR remodels happen in occupied locations. We phase work around peak meal times, build dust and noise containment, and hand areas back to operations before the next shift opens.

Portfolio Consistency

Running 20 locations simultaneously requires systems, not just ambition. Our program management infrastructure keeps every site on the same cadence with the same quality bar.
