## 1. Executive Snapshot

RJB is a national commercial general contractor headquartered in Spring City, Pennsylvania. The company positions itself around two core service lines—Construction and Asset Maintenance—serving retailers, grocery chains, restaurants, and other commercial brands. RJB emphasizes end-to-end accountability, practical execution, strong communication, safety, and the ability to manage repeatable multi-site work as well as larger, more customized projects.

Internally, RJB operates as a program-driven, workflow-heavy commercial contractor with strong repeat-business characteristics. Much of the business is organized around named client programs, standardized SOPs, recurring project types, structured handoffs, and multi-system coordination across estimating, operations, project administration, field delivery, closeout, and billing.

In simple terms:

- Externally, RJB is a one-stop commercial contracting partner.
- Operationally, RJB is a high-coordination execution business built around process discipline, client-specific program rules, and cross-functional delivery.
- Strategically, RJB's biggest leverage comes from improving visibility, reducing duplication, and making structured knowledge easier to use across departments.

## 2. Company Identity and Positioning

### Official public-facing positioning

RJB is a nationally licensed commercial general contractor that helps retailers, grocery chains, restaurants, and other commercial brands execute renovations, rollouts, fit-outs, and maintenance programs across the country. The company takes responsibility for the full construction process from inception to completion and complements its construction capability with full-service asset maintenance offerings.

### What "integrated" means in practice

RJB delivers across the full project lifecycle under one operating umbrella:

- RJB handles both project-based construction and ongoing asset / facility-related maintenance work.
- RJB manages preconstruction, estimating, buyout, job setup, subcontract administration, field execution, documentation, closeout, and invoicing.
- The business supports both single-project execution and repeatable national client programs.
- The company frequently serves as the coordinating layer between client, site leadership, subcontractors, vendors, and internal teams.

### Public operating promise

RJB's brand promise is "construction without surprises." In practice that means:

- solve difficult contracting problems,
- execute reliably,
- communicate clearly,
- maintain quality and safety,
- and handle complexity without pushing chaos back onto the client.

RJB's recurring positioning principle: it builds *with* clients, not just *for* them—adapting to how each client operates rather than forcing clients into a fixed process.

RJB earns its place in the market through a mix of the following:

### A. Program-based work for national or regional clients

## 9. Delivery Model: How Work Flows Through RJB

The SOPs show a repeatable operating pattern that generally looks like this:

### 1. Opportunity / bid intake
- Lead or opportunity created
- Client/program identified
- Estimating and/or business development engaged
- Early tracking set up in Acumatica / estimating overhead structure

### 2. Preconstruction / survey / proposal
- Site visit or desk review performed
- Sub coverage sourced
- Proposal assembled
- Client-specific documents uploaded or submitted through required platform

### 3. Award and internal setup
- Job numbers created in Acumatica
- Folder structures created in SharePoint
- Smartsheet program and/or project trackers created
- Budgets entered
- PM / Project Administrator / Superintendent assignments made

### 4. Buyout and procurement
- Subcontractors selected and de-scoped
- Subcontracts issued through DocuSign / ACM workflows
- Purchase orders created
- Materials and equipment ordered

### 5. Field mobilization and execution
- Preconstruction meeting held
- Schedule communicated
- Field reporting begins
- Daily or frequent photo documentation required
- Store / client communication maintained

### 6. Punch and closeout
- PM / client / manufacturer / third-party walkthroughs occur
- Punch lists generated and completed
- Signoff forms, warranties, redlines, and other closeout items gathered
- Completion docs uploaded to required systems

### 7. Billing and financial close
- Revenue budget updated
- Proforma created in Acumatica
- Client-system billing completed where required
- Change orders processed and billed
- Final paperwork archived

This is a company where the workflow is itself a core product. RJB is not only selling labor and subcontractor coordination; it is selling consistent execution across a lot of moving parts.

## 12. Systems Landscape

RJB's workflows are system-dependent. The most repeatedly visible platforms in the SOPs are:

### Core internal systems
- **Acumatica (ACM)** — job setup, budgets, financial structure, subcontracts, POs, change orders, billing/proformas, customer/vendor records
- **SharePoint** — primary document storage, program folders, estimating folders, project files, photo storage, closeout archives
- **Smartsheet** — program trackers, project trackers, CO logs, vendor lists, status dashboards, milestone visibility
- **DocuSign** — subcontract and change order execution
- **Procore** — used on certain construction workflows for project records, meetings, daily logs, punch, documents, and reporting

### Frequent external or client-facing systems seen in SOPs
- Service Channel
- T-Builder
- Scopeworker
- ProTrack / PMWA / Ariba variants depending on client program
- Weis FTP
- WorkDocs
- Hotel Engine (travel support)

### What this means operationally
RJB does not run on one platform. It runs on a **system mesh**:

- ERP / finance structure in Acumatica
- file structure in SharePoint
- tracker logic in Smartsheet
- execution records partly in Procore
- plus client-specific external systems for intake, reporting, compliance, and invoicing

This is one of the most important truths about RJB as a business.

## 13. Operating Characteristics That Define RJB

## A. Program discipline matters
Many clients have unique rules, document sets, billing triggers, naming conventions, reporting requirements, and closeout standards. RJB's value depends heavily on remembering and following them.

## B. Administrative precision matters almost as much as field execution
The business is not just "build the work." It is also:

- enter the right data,
- save the right files,
- send the right contract,
- log the right note,
- upload the right proof,
- and invoice the right way.

## C. Repeatability is a competitive advantage
RJB is strongest where work can be standardized into playbooks, templates, trackers, and reusable SOPs.

## D. Communication is not optional
Public materials emphasize communication, and the SOPs confirm why. A breakdown in communication causes schedule drift, missing documentation, budget confusion, delayed billing, missed client expectations, or rework.

## E. Visual documentation is central
Photos, punch photos, survey photos, progress pictures, completion pictures, signoffs, and PDF/PPT packaging appear repeatedly. RJB is a documentation-heavy operator.

## F. Client trust is built through execution clarity
RJB's best-fit clients value dependable execution, repeatable process, and a contractor that can operate cleanly inside program constraints.

## 15. Internal Friction Patterns Implied by the SOP Set

The source set implies several structural friction points common to RJB's operating model:

### Fragmentation across tools
Critical workflow data is spread across multiple platforms and often must be re-entered.

### Knowledge lives in SOPs, email habits, and role memory
A lot of process knowledge is documented, but still depends on experienced people remembering how each client/program works.

### Program variation creates hidden complexity
Even when project types look similar, billing, closeout, approval, client communication, and system requirements differ by client.

### Coordination overhead is high
The business relies on PMs, Project Administrators, supers, estimating, accounting, IT, and subs all staying aligned.

### Documentation load is substantial
Closeout and invoicing readiness depend on many small required artifacts being complete and correct.

These are not signs of weakness. They are signs of a real operating business with complexity that has outgrown informal, memory-based process.
